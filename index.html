The Art of Staying in Touch

A Letter from Paper Hearts Club

I've always believed there is something special about creating things you can actually hold. In a world where so much of our lives exist on screens and disappear as quickly as we scroll past them, there is something grounding about paper. A handwritten note, a postcard tucked inside a book, a sheet of stationery you've been saving for the right moment, or a letter that lives in a drawer for years before you stumble across it again. These are the kinds of things that become part of our lives instead of simply passing through them.

Paper Hearts Club was created to celebrate that feeling. It's a monthly invitation to slow down, to make something with your hands, to collect beautiful paper goods that deserve to be used instead of saved forever, and to remember that creativity doesn't have to live behind a screen. Sometimes the most meaningful things we create are the ones we fold into an envelope and send to someone else.

Our Pegasus is a reminder that imagination and connection have always traveled farther than distance. Some of the people we love live around the corner, while others are hundreds of miles away, but a letter has a way of making that distance feel smaller.

Every envelope carries more than paper. It carries the time someone set aside to think of another person, to share a story, celebrate a milestone, offer encouragement, or simply say, “I was thinking about you.” Those are the things that stay with us long after the envelope has been opened.

I hope these letters encourage you to create more, write more, save more, and surround yourself with tangible reminders of the people and moments that matter most.
