Collected & Created

I'm a graphic designer by trade, but I've always been someone who creates simply because I love the process of making things.

Long before Paper Hearts, I was collecting postcards on trips, hunting for beautiful stationery I could never quite bring myself to use, saving wrapping paper that felt too pretty to throw away, and filling drawers with little paper treasures that somehow made everyday life feel more meaningful.

Design has taught me to appreciate thoughtful details, beautiful typography, and the way something as simple as a piece of paper can make someone stop, smile, and feel something. While so much of my career revolves around creating for screens, I've always found myself returning to the tactile side of creativity—the things you can fold, write on, pin to a bulletin board, tuck inside a book, or keep in a box for years.

Paper Hearts grew out of that love.

I wanted to create the kind of mail I would be excited to find in my own mailbox every month, a collection of thoughtfully designed paper goods, creative projects, handwritten inspiration, and little surprises that encourage people to slow down and create something tangible.

My hope is that every envelope reminds you that creativity doesn't have to be complicated, and that the most meaningful things we make are often the ones we share with someone else.

Thank you for being here and for helping keep the art of handwritten letters alive.
